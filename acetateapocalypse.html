An Acetate Apocalypse!

Hello everyone!

I’m back again to share a little story about my acetate troubles from my university days! While calling it an “acetate apocalypse” might sound dramatic, I aimed for a title with the impact of a blockbuster. The alliteration not only adds emphasis but also makes it more memorable.

Storytime: Acetate Conundrums

The cake’s theme was a pink showstopper, and I thought, what better way to capture that than with a Great Gatsby twist? Back in my uni days, I didn’t experiment with external aids—no stencils, molds, or transfer papers; it was all about pure originality with hand piping, sprinkles, and, of course, a whole lot of glitter. This time, I decided to go big or go home (after all, isn’t that what Gatsby was all about?). I fired up good old TikTok, searched for “Great Gatsby design,” and soon found myself down the rabbit hole of “acetate tutorials.”

The designs were so beautiful that I thought, why not give it a shot myself?

I prepared my pink buttercream and had a cold cake ready to go, following those 30-second TikTok videos, letting them loop over 20 times. What could possibly go wrong, right? Once I placed the stencil on the cake, it wouldn’t stick (problem 1). So, I thought, why not use some sewing pins? Don’t worry, they were disinfected and clean—5-star hygiene all the way! I carefully laid on the pink buttercream, ensuring it wasn’t too thin and pressing down hard but also taking my time with the process. It looked good, but I was nervous about what was coming next.!

Now it was time to scrape off the excess buttercream—easy peasy, right? Nope! (problem 2) I may have been scraping while using my hands to hold the acetate, so when I pulled the plastic away, wait for it—the white buttercream base also came off where my hands had been positioned. At 2 AM in my university house, I was fighting the urge to scream and curse in frustration.

Determined not to start over (especially since the base was white and the pink buttercream would stain it), I decided to think outside the box. I quickly applied white buttercream to some areas and smoothed it out. For the less salvageable spots, I piped some cute buttercream rosettes. High IQ move at 2 AM, I know!

In the end, it all turned out well—if it hadn’t, you wouldn’t see the picture above!

Reflections and Takeaways

This experience taught me the importance of patience and adaptability in the kitchen. Sometimes, things don’t go as planned, but with a little creativity and quick thinking, you can turn a potential disaster into a stunning success.

Thank you for tuning into my university baking stories! Stay tuned for more updates, and as always, feel free to share your thoughts or ask questions in the comments. Here’s to another week of creativity and deliciousness!

Happy baking and crafting!

Kezia xoxo
